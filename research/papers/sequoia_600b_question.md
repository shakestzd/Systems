# AI's $600B Question

**Author:** David Cahn (Sequoia Capital)
**Date:** June 20, 2024
**Source:** [Sequoia Capital Blog](https://www.sequoiacap.com/article/ais-600b-question/)

## The Core Argument
In September 2023, David Cahn wrote "AI’s $200B Question." Nine months later, the number has tripled to $600B. This refers to the massive gap between the revenue required to justify the AI infrastructure buildout (CapEx) and the actual revenue being generated by AI companies.

## Key Points
1. **The Revenue Gap:** The "revenue requirement" (the amount of revenue needed to pay for the GPUs, data centers, and power) has ballooned. Cahn estimates the industry needs to generate $600B in revenue to justify current investment levels.
2. **GPU Supply:** Nvidia is the primary beneficiary, but the massive scale of GPU purchasing (led by Big Tech) is creating a potential overcapacity risk if applications don't materialize fast enough.
3. **Speculative Frenzy vs. Real Value:** While AI is a transformative technology, the current investment cycle resembles previous infrastructure bubbles (like the dot-com fiber boom).
4. **The "Wait and See" Danger:** Cahn argues that "the $600B question is not: when will the bubble burst? It is: how do we create $600B of value?"

## SD Relevance
This is a critical piece for modeling the **CapEx -> Infrastructure Demand** feedback loop. It highlights the risk of a "Boom-Bust" cycle in data center and energy infrastructure if the commercial payoff is delayed.
