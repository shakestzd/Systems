# Publication Architecture — TZD Labs Systems Research

## What This Publication Is

Technology capital is reshaping the physical world faster than any analytical framework tracks. AI companies commit hundreds of billions to data centers. Utilities cite data center load growth to justify new generation. Chip fabs receive federal subsidies that reshape regional labor markets for a generation. These decisions happen in separate rooms. But they are building the same physical world, and their interactions create obligations that persist long after the investment thesis that justified them.

This publication investigates where technology capital lands in the physical economy — what it locks in, who builds it, and who bears the consequences when demand doesn't materialize as projected.

**We start where Politano ends.** He shows the scale of investment and asks whether it will pay off, then stops. We ask what that capital is actually buying: which physical assets, in which communities, on what timeline, with what durability, and who holds the long-term liability if the demand thesis doesn't materialize.

## The Analytical Arc

Every deep dive traces the same chain:

```
COMMIT → CONVERT → LAND → DISTRIBUTE

Commit:      Financial commitment (hyperscaler capex, project finance, PPAs, policy subsidy)
Convert:     What physical asset? Durability class? (Structural / Policy-dependent / DTD)
Land:        Where — geography, grid node, utility territory, labor market
Distribute:  Who benefits? Who bears long-term cost if demand thesis misses?
```

The chain is not linear in practice. Capital flows trigger regulatory responses that alter where the next wave of capital lands. Policy creates incentives that bring in new capital, which creates new land-use and labor-market consequences. Every deep dive maps the full loop, not just the entry point.

---

## Scope

The analytical framework is portable across technology capital stories. AI infrastructure is the current primary focus because it is the largest driver of technology capital in the physical economy. The governing questions apply equally to energy transition capital, semiconductor manufacturing subsidies, and climate finance — wherever financial commitments convert into durable physical assets.

**Current deep dives:**

- **AI infrastructure** — data centers, compute hardware, power demand (DD-001–004)
- **Energy transition** — generation mix, grid modernization, PPA mechanics (DD-002)
- **Climate finance** — how capital intersects clean energy policy (DD-002, future)
- **Materials supply chains** — GOES, copper, critical minerals (CS-4, future)

## Governing Questions Per Deep Dive

The governing question is an internal compass — not the published title. It answers "what is this piece actually about?" before the analysis begins.

**DD-001 | Capital Reality**
How much of declared AI investment converts to durable physical assets, on what timeline does each dollar become irreversible, and what do current valuations imply?

**DD-002 | Grid Modernization**
Which grid assets does AI capital build, who finances them, and who owns the stranded risk when demand forecasts miss?

**DD-003 | Labor Markets**
Where do displaced workers go, and what wage penalty does the transition to available work impose?

**DD-004 | Utility Regulation**
Through what regulatory mechanisms does AI infrastructure capital convert to ratepayer obligations, and who decides the allocation?

**CS-4 | Materials**
Which material constraints take 10+ years to resolve, and who controls the chokepoints?

## Title Conventions

Published titles should be claims or tensions, not category labels.

| Type | Example |
| :--- | :--- |
| Label (weak) | "AI Capital and Grid Modernization" |
| Claim (strong) | "The Grid as AI Infrastructure: Who Builds It, Who Pays For It" |
| Question (strong) | "What Does a 20-Year Power Purchase Agreement Actually Lock In?" |

**Pattern for piece titles:** `[Claim or central tension] — [distributional question or stakes]`

**Pattern for chart H1 (the finding):** state the insight, not the chart type.

| Type | Example |
| :--- | :--- |
| Chart type (weak) | "AI Capex by Company, 2020–2024" |
| Insight (strong) | "Hyperscaler capex doubled in two years and is still accelerating" |

## Reader Contract

The reader already knows AI investment is large and accelerating — Politano tells them that. They come here because they want to know what that capital is actually buying, whether the physical delivery matches the financial commitment, and who ends up holding the risk. They can read a utility rate filing and understand what it means. They are not served by any current publication.

Write for the person who has read Politano and is asking: "okay, so what does $1 trillion actually become?"
