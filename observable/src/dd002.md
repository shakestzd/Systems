# 40-Year Energy Decisions on 3-Year Demand Forecasts

Since 2020, the U.S. has added ${stats.total_gw} GW of new generation capacity. AI demand did not start this buildout. Clean energy economics and the IRA did. But AI companies are now making 40-year energy decisions on 3-year demand forecasts.

## AI demand forecasts span 3 years. The infrastructure lasts 25 to 50.

Every capital decision being made today creates a multi-decade infrastructure consequence from a near-term demand thesis. A gas combined cycle plant built for AI load in 2025 will still be running in 2060. The demand signal that funded it may not last five years.

Virginia is the canary. What is happening there represents an early version of the tension every state with significant data center growth will face: Texas, Georgia, Ohio, Arizona. The policy response in Virginia will set precedent.

The picture is not entirely one-sided. Amazon commissioned and paid for this study; its conclusion is the opposite of the independent UCS analysis. Whether data centers are net cost-shifters or net contributors depends on rate design, load profile, and the specific utility territory.

## The Feedback Architecture

Five feedback loops determine whether AI capital modernizes the grid for everyone or builds private infrastructure for a few.

```js

display(mountScrollChart(cldChart.node, cldChart.update, [
  { prose: "Five feedback loops determine the outcome. Two reinforcing (R1, R2), three balancing (B1-B3)." },
  { prose: "R1 (blue): The Grid Investment Cycle. AI demand flows through the grid, infrastructure improves, costs fall, more demand connects." },
  { prose: "R2 (green): The Renewable Learning Curve. More PPAs drive more deployment, which drives cost down, which drives more PPAs." },
  { prose: "B2 (red): BTM Bypass. Long queues make behind-the-meter attractive. No grid investment. Ratepayers absorb costs. Political backlash follows." },
  { prose: "The critical variable is Regulatory Environment. It determines which loops dominate. Drag nodes to explore the structure." },
], { callout: "above" }));
```

The reinforcing loops (R1, R2) drive grid modernization and renewable cost reduction. The balancing loops (B1, B2, B3) resist it through regulatory uncertainty, behind-the-meter bypass, and political backlash. The critical variable is regulatory environment: it influences both the attractiveness of grid connection and the attractiveness of bypass.

### Baseline Dynamics

A systems dynamics model (PySD, Vensim) formalizes these loops. Under baseline parameters, three dynamics emerge: grid capacity grows, but behind-the-meter grows faster; the queue slowly clears; and renewable costs keep falling. Once the system tips into the capture regime, behind-the-meter infrastructure is built and sunk, and the political constituency for grid investment shrinks. The infrastructure will outlast the regulatory moment that shapes it.

## Sources & Methodology

**EIA generation data.** EIA Form 860 (2024 release), monthly and annual generator inventories. Nameplate capacity only; does not reflect capacity factor differences across fuel types. Capacity factors are national EIA averages (solar: 25%, gas CC: 57%, wind: 35%, nuclear: 93%).

**Interconnection queue data.** Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, *Queued Up: Characteristics of Power Plants Seeking Transmission Interconnection* (2025 edition). Regional breakdowns from LBNL supplementary tables.

**Cost allocation.** Union of Concerned Scientists, *Data Center Demand and the Grid: Quantifying Cost Socialization in PJM* (September 2025). The $${stats.ucs_cost_bn}B figure covers PJM data center transmission connection costs approved in 2024 and passed to ratepayers.

**Virginia bill impact.** Joint Legislative Audit and Review Commission (JLARC) / Energy + Environmental Economics (E3), *Review of Data Center Impacts on Virginia's Electric Grid* (December 2024). High-growth scenario endpoint of $${stats.va_bill_annual}/year by 2040.

**Systems dynamics model.** Grid modernization model (Vensim/PySD) with 5 stocks, ~20 parameters. The model is exploratory, not predictive — it identifies which feedback loops dominate under different parameter regimes. Parameters are order-of-magnitude assumptions. The sensitivity analysis sweeps parameter ranges rather than relying on point estimates.

**Regulatory events.** FERC Order 2023 (interconnection reform, in effect). FERC Docket EL25-49-000 (co-located load cost allocation in PJM, December 2025). FERC Order 1920 (transmission planning, under 8th Circuit legal challenge as of February 2026).

*All data from public, freely accessible sources. Analysis: TZD Labs, March 2026.*
